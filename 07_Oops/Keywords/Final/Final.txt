                                                            Final keyword in java

- The final keyword in Java is used to apply restrictions on variables, methods, and classes.
- Final class can't be inherited.
- Final method can't be overridden
- Final varable is like constant can't be updated



                                                Inheritance methods & final

- Inherited Methods: Methods defined in the superclass and directly available to the subclass without changes.
- Overridden Methods: Methods defined in the superclass and redefined in the subclass. The subclass provides its specific implementation.
- Child-Specific Methods: Methods defined only in the subclass, providing behaviors unique to the subclass.
- Final Variables: A constant that cannot be changed once assigned
- Final Methods: Methods that cannot be overridden by subclasses.
- Final Classes: Classes that cannot be subclassed.
